Search Engine Journal has published a recap and registration page for an on-demand session titled "The New Rules of Search," covering AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) and content marketing trends aimed at 2026. The session was presented by Shannon Vize (Sr. Content Marketing Manager at Conductor) and Pat Reinhart (VP of Services & Thought Leadership at Conductor).

## What's actually new

The session focuses on three areas: identifying which content types and AEO tactics have the highest chance of generating AI citations, reframing KPIs for a landscape where AI platforms capture user intent before a site visit ever happens, and using agentic workflows to produce authority-building content at scale. The framing here is practical — it's pitched as a "prioritized framework" for shifting content investment toward visibility in AI-driven search experiences rather than traditional click-through metrics. The source doesn't publish specific benchmarks or tool names; the detail lives behind the webinar registration wall. This is a Conductor-produced session, so expect the lens to be shaped by their product ecosystem.

## What it means for your config

This isn't a developer tooling release, and there are no config files, CLI changes, or schema updates to account for. That said, if your team manages structured data configs (JSON-LD schemas, meta tags, or CMS-level SEO templates), the broader AEO trend is worth tracking. As AI search engines increasingly pull structured answers rather than linking out, how you mark up content — and what content formats you prioritize — may start influencing your site's build and config pipelines. The session doesn't detail specific technical implementations or markup changes, so there's nothing concrete to act on from a config perspective today. We'll revisit if Conductor or SEJ publish technical guidance with specific schema or markup recommendations.

## Recommended next step

If your role touches content strategy or you maintain the structured data layer of a marketing site, watch the on-demand session to see whether Conductor's framework maps to anything actionable in your stack. Pay particular attention to the "agentic workflows" portion — if they name specific tools or integration patterns, that's where config-level changes are most likely to surface. For pure infrastructure teams, this is a "keep an eye on it" topic, not a "drop everything" one.
